(Umeå Institute of Design (UID))

On discovering the dying plantwall, Danielle Wilde, UID Professor in Design for Sustainability, seized the opportunity to foster new collaborations across UmU, SLU and beyond. The original idea was to explore the vertical garden as a site for fostering human-plant interactions in the Arctic region, with a focus on the Boreal Forest. The idea evolved organically, influenced by collaborators, supporters, and the unbounded creativity of the students, working in and across disciplinary teams.


(UID Climate Group)
The UID Climate Group is a student initiative from Umeå Institute of Design. Its main goal is to unite students to learn to become a designer for a sustainable future.


(UmU Dept. of Molecular Biology / Umeå Centre for Microbial Research (UCMR))
The death and rebirth of a vertical garden by Hanna Forsberg, Rana Zeeb, Teresa Frisan and Javier Avila Cariño. This project is the bachelor thesis work of Hanna Forsberg and Rana Zeeb. HF and RZ develop most of the experimental set-up and carry on all experimental work. TF and JAC performmentoring support. Financing: UmU Departmentof Molecular Biology.


(SLU, Faculty of Forest Sciences / Dept. of Forest Genetics and Plant Physiology, Umeå)
M Rosario García Gil, Lecturer, Dept. of Forest Genetics and Plant Physiology; Vice Dean, internationalisation, Faculty of Forest Sciences. García Gil led development and installation of the plant wall demonstrators for the Växtvägg project. García Gil's research is focused on forest genetics and the physiology of Boreal forests, where her main contribution has been to decipher the genetics of forests' adaptation to the local conditions and to integrate remote sensing into assisted adaptation models. Her determination to bring boreal forests closer to cities led to her and her company's participation in the project.


(SLU, School for Forest Management, Skinnskatteberg)
Seedlings cultivated at the Swedish University of Agricultural Sciences (SLU), School for Forest Management in Skinnskatteberg by the following students: Annica Berglind, Ninja Bernzen, Rickard Bodell and Emanuel Vestlund, under the supervision of Elisabeth Wallin and Daniel Gräns.


(Vertisà)
Founded by M Rosario García Gil, Vertisà is a company that has developed a system to cultivate vertically local flora and fauna in the cities, indoors and outdoors, inspired by García Gil's research into boreal forests and fascination for green urban architecture.
/ M Rosario García Gil, founder
/ Juha Niemi, marketing and engineering
/ Jose Grases, design and prototyping
/ Ulrika Forsberg, garden designer
/ Jordi Serramia, expert in indoor vertical gardening.
Vertisà has been listed for three consecutive years on the IVAs-100 list. In 2021, Vertisà won the SKAPA innovation award. Vertisà has led the development and installation of the vertical gardens installed at the UmU Arts Campus, April and May 2023, working in close collaboration with the UID Climate Group.
